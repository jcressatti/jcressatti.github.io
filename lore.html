The Angler’s Revel

At dawn, I greet the silvered lake,
With jigheads bright in chartreuse wake,
The morning’s charm in every cast,
Hopes hooked on lines that hold them fast.

Shiners dance, not chubs today,
In cool clear depths where shadows play.
Each lure a sunburst, green and gold,
A story in the waters told.

When sun ascends its highest seat,
My line in waters twelve feet deep,
Yet tales and old-time anglers swear,
The giants lurk in shallows near.

Come afternoon, a fiery gleam,
My jigs now dressed in sunset's theme.
Orange flashes beneath the blue,
A vibrant chase, an old game new.

Each tug a verse, each catch a cheer,
With friends along, the party's here.
We toast the depths, the day, the fish,
In every wave, a hearty wish.

As evening falls, our laughter rings,
Around the fire, where someone sings.
We revel 'neath the starlit dome,
In nature’s heart, we find our home.

So here’s to hooks, to lines, to bait,
To all the tales that fishermen state.
For every cast brings joy anew,
In the party of the morning dew.
